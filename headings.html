The Heading Element

Headings are usually a word or a phrase at the beginning of a paragraph or article to give the reader some context of what they are about to read. In HTML, that is essentially what they are used for as well. HTML 5 provides six different headings to choose from. The way headings are written in HTML is between two brackets, like this: < and >. This is the structure of most HTML elements actually. Then, you just input the level heading you want to use. Heading 1 (h1) is commonly used to represent the most important heading on the webpage, followed by 2, (h2) and then all the way to 6 (h6). 6 being the least important. Headings are not only for the website user's benefit though, they are also used by programmers and by the browser to organize their document in an outline format.

While that is how all the different level headings are pre-programmed by HTML, there is a way to adjust the font size and the font weight (boldness) of any text. So you can really make any text look any way you want. I could make a heading using h6, but program it to be the same size and boldness as h1 if I wanted.

To learn more about this element, you can go to the official W3C Website: HTML Headings
